🚀 Research Interests & Future Directions

🎯 Primary Research Areas

🧠 Computational Neuroscience

Developing biologically-plausible models of neural computation with focus on prefrontal cortex functions, action planning mechanisms, and the integration of short-term synaptic plasticity with reservoir computing architectures.

Future Goals: Unraveling the mathematical principles underlying biological intelligence and applying these insights to create more efficient artificial neural networks.

⚡ Efficient AI Architectures

Creating lightweight transformer architectures inspired by biological neural networks, focusing on novel weight-sharing mechanisms and adaptive learning strategies that maintain performance while dramatically reducing computational requirements.

Future Goals: Developing AI systems that can operate efficiently on resource-constrained devices while maintaining state-of-the-art performance.

🤖 Large Language Models

Advancing the theoretical foundations and practical applications of large language models through reservoir computing principles, distributed training optimization, and novel approaches to computational efficiency in NLP systems.

Future Goals: Building next-generation language models that combine biological plausibility with practical scalability for real-world applications.

🌟 Emerging Research Frontiers

🎯 Goal-Oriented AI Systems

Developing AI systems capable of complex planning and decision-making through bio-inspired mechanisms, bridging computational neuroscience with practical applications in autonomous systems and intelligent agents.

🔄 Adaptive Learning Systems

Creating self-organizing neural structures that adapt dynamically to different tasks and environments, incorporating principles of meta-learning and continual learning for robust AI systems.

🌐 Interdisciplinary AI Applications

Exploring applications of bio-inspired AI in diverse fields including healthcare, education, robotics, and scientific computing, with focus on creating practical solutions for complex real-world problems.
